# 🏔️👭 The Legend of the Tararua Ranges 👭🏔️

Long ago, there were two sisters named **Tararua** and **Ruarua**. They were the daughters of a great chief and were known for their strength and determination. When their father died, the sisters inherited his land—a wide, flat plain between the mountains and the sea.

The sisters loved their land, but they often argued about how to care for it. Tararua wanted to build great villages and cultivate the soil. Ruarua wanted to keep the land wild, with forests and birds.

---

## 🗣️ A Disagreement

Their arguments grew louder over the years. The people of the land did not know whom to follow. Some sided with Tararua, others with Ruarua. The sisters’ relationship became strained, and they stopped speaking to each other.

One day, Tararua said, “If you will not work with me, then I will go where I can build my villages without you.” She walked toward the mountains, carrying her share of the land on her shoulders.

---

## 🏔️ The Separation

As Tararua walked, the land beneath her rose into great peaks. She climbed higher and higher, until she stood at the top of a mighty range.

Ruarua watched her sister go. She was sad but stubborn. She walked toward the sea, and the land beneath her formed rolling hills and valleys. She made her home by the coast, where the forests grew thick and the birds sang freely.

The mountains between them became known as the **Tararua Ranges**, and the lowlands by the sea were called **Ruarua**.

---

## 💡 What This Story Teaches

This story reminds us:

> Different ideas can both be valuable, even when they lead people apart.  
> The landscape is shaped by the choices of our ancestors.  
> Sometimes separation is needed for each to flourish in their own way.
