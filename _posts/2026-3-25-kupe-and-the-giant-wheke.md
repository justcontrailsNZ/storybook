# 🌊🐙 Kupe and the Giant Wheke 🐙🌊

The great explorer **Kupe** is famous for discovering Aotearoa (New Zealand). But before he reached these shores, he had to defeat a fearsome enemy—a giant wheke (octopus) that terrorised his homeland.

---

## 🐚 The Wheke’s Attack

Kupe lived in Hawaiki, the ancestral homeland of Māori. One day, a giant octopus called **Te Wheke-a-Muturangi** began attacking fishing canoes. It wrapped its long tentacles around boats and dragged them under. Fishermen were afraid to go to sea.

Kupe decided he had to stop the wheke. He built a strong canoe and gathered a crew. He told them, “We will chase this monster to the ends of the ocean.”

---

## ⛵ The Great Chase

Kupe and his crew sailed across the vast Pacific Ocean. The wheke was fast, but Kupe was determined. He used his knowledge of stars, currents, and birds to follow its trail.

For weeks they chased. The ocean grew rough. Some of his crew wanted to turn back, but Kupe refused.

Finally, they cornered the wheke near the shores of a new land—Aotearoa.

---

## ⚔️ The Battle

The giant wheke rose from the water, its tentacles thrashing. Kupe stood at the bow of his canoe, holding a sharpened weapon.

As the wheke reached out, Kupe struck. He fought bravely, and with the help of his crew, they defeated the beast.

The wheke sank beneath the waves, and the sea became calm.

---

## 🌏 Discovering Aotearoa

Kupe and his crew landed on the new land. They explored its mountains, rivers, and forests. Kupe named many places, including **Te Whanganui-a-Tara** (Wellington Harbour) and **Te Awa Kairangi** (Hutt River).

He returned to Hawaiki with stories of the new land. Later, many others would follow, settling in Aotearoa.

---

## 💡 What This Story Teaches

This story reminds us:

> Courage and determination can overcome great challenges.  
> Exploration takes patience and skill.  
> Protecting your people sometimes means facing danger.
