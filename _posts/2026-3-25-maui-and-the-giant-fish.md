# 🎣🐟 Māui and the Giant Fish 🐟🎣

Māui was known for his clever tricks and bold adventures. One day, he decided he wanted to go fishing with his brothers. But his brothers always left without him because they thought he was too young and too cheeky.

Māui hid in the bottom of their waka (canoe) under some old fishing nets. When they were far out to sea, he jumped out.

“Where did you come from?” his brothers shouted.

“I want to fish with you,” Māui said with a grin.

They grumbled but let him stay.

---

## 🪝 A Magic Hook

Māui pulled out his fishing hook. It was not an ordinary hook. It was made from the jawbone of his grandmother and was filled with magic.

The brothers gave Māui the worst piece of bait—a bit of old shell. But Māui simply took it and chanted a karakia (prayer).

He cast his hook into the deep ocean.

The hook sank down, down, down… until it touched something enormous.

---

## 🌊 A Giant Bite

Suddenly, the ocean began to churn. The waka shook violently. Māui held tight to his line and pulled with all his strength.

“He has hooked something!” cried the brothers. “Cut the line! It will pull us under!”

But Māui kept pulling.

“I have hooked the son of the sea god,” Māui said. “And I will not let go.”

He pulled and pulled. Slowly, a great shape rose from the water. It was not a fish—it was a giant, a **ika** (fish) so huge it seemed like an island.

The brothers stared in awe as the giant fish surfaced. It was bigger than any they had ever seen.

---

## 🏔️ The North Island

Māui’s brothers, still afraid, began to cut pieces of the fish for themselves. They argued and fought over the best parts.

Māui was disappointed. He had wanted to offer the fish to the gods, but his brothers’ greed had spoiled the gift.

In some versions of the story, the fish became the North Island of Aotearoa. The mountains and valleys were formed by the brothers’ cuts. The canoe became the South Island, and Māui’s hook became the curved coastline of Hawke’s Bay.

---

## 💡 What This Story Teaches

This story teaches us:

> Patience and respect are important when receiving a gift.  
> Greed can ruin something beautiful.  
> The land itself can be shaped by the actions and choices of people.

## 📖 Quick Glossary

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **waka** | a canoe, often used for fishing or travel |
| **karakia** | a prayer or chant |
| **ika** | the Māori word for fish |
| **greed** | wanting more than you need, without thinking of others |
