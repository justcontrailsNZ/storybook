title: "Demeter and Persephone"
---

Long ago, the goddess Demeter watched over farming and harvests. She helped wheat grow tall, fruit ripen on trees, and vegetables sprout in gardens.

Demeter cared deeply about people, because people needed food to live.

But the most important person in Demeter’s life was her daughter, Persephone.

Persephone was kind and curious. She loved walking through fields and picking flowers.

Persephone disappears
One sunny day, Persephone went into a meadow to gather blooms. The sky was bright, and bees hummed softly.

But suddenly, Persephone disappeared.

Demeter heard nothing at first. Then she noticed the meadow felt strangely quiet.

When Demeter couldn’t find her daughter, she became frightened.

Demeter searched everywhere.

She walked through forests. She climbed hills. She asked rivers and trees if they had seen Persephone. She travelled for days, then weeks.

But no one could tell her where Persephone was.

The world changes
Demeter was filled with grief. Her sadness became so heavy that she stopped doing her work.

She stopped helping crops grow.

At first, people didn’t notice. Then plants began to droop. Leaves turned brown. Fields that should have been green became dry.

Farmers stared at their land, worried. “Why isn’t anything growing?” they asked.

Children felt hungry. Families grew scared.

The world was changing because Demeter’s heart was broken.

The truth is discovered
At last, Demeter learned what had happened.

Persephone had been taken to the Underworld to live there with its ruler for part of the year.

Demeter was furious and devastated.

“She must come back,” Demeter demanded. “The world cannot survive like this.”

The gods could see that humans were suffering too. They knew something had to change.

The agreement
A deal was made.

Persephone would return to Demeter—but there was one problem. In Greek myths, if someone ate food in the Underworld, they could not leave forever.

Persephone had eaten a small amount of food there.

So the agreement became this:

Persephone would spend part of each year in the Underworld.
And she would spend the rest of the year on Earth with Demeter.
Demeter did not like it, but she agreed, because it meant she would see her daughter again.

How seasons begin
When Persephone returned to Demeter, Demeter’s joy burst out across the land.

Green shoots sprang up. Trees budded. Flowers opened. Farms became full again.

This was spring.

As Persephone stayed, the world grew warm and bright. Crops grew tall. Fruit ripened.

This was summer.

But each year, the time came for Persephone to return to the Underworld.

Demeter’s sadness returned too. The air cooled. Leaves fell. Fields became bare.

This was autumn and winter.

Then Persephone returned again, and the cycle started over.

Why people remember this myth
This Greek myth helped people explain something they saw every year: the changing seasons.

It also reminds us how love and emotions can shape our actions—and how important it is to care for one another, especially in hard times.
