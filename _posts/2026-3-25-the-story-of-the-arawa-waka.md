# 🛶🔥 The Story of the Arawa Waka 🔥🛶

The **Arawa** waka is one of the most famous of the great migration canoes. It was led by **Tama-te-kapua**, a chief known for his cunning and bravery. The canoe carried the people who would become the Te Arawa confederation of tribes in the Bay of Plenty.

The journey from Hawaiki was not smooth. Tama-te-kapua stole food and caused trouble, but he also showed great skill in navigation. The waka was guided by the stars and by the shark-shaped clouds that appeared ahead.

---

## 🏝️ Landing at Maketū

The Arawa made landfall at **Maketū** in the Bay of Plenty. There, Tama-te-kapua and his people began to explore. They travelled inland, discovering the lakes of Rotorua and the geothermal wonders.

One of the most famous stories from the Arawa is that of **Ngātoro-i-rangi**, the high priest who climbed Mount Tongariro and summoned fire to save himself from freezing.

---

## 🌋 The Legacy of Fire

Ngātoro-i-rangi’s journey brought fire to the central plateau, creating the geothermal areas that define the region. The descendants of the Arawa became guardians of these places, passing down the stories of their ancestors.

The Arawa waka is still honoured today. Its carved prow sits in a museum, and the iwi of Te Arawa continue to celebrate their heritage.

---

## 💡 What This Story Teaches

This story teaches:

> Leadership requires both skill and wisdom.  
> The journeys of our ancestors shaped the land we live on.  
> We are connected to the past through stories and places.
