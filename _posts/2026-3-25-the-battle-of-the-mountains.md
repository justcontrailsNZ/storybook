# 🏔️⚔️ The Battle of the Mountains ⚔️🏔️

Long ago, the great mountains of the North Island lived together in the central plateau. **Tongariro**, **Taranaki**, **Ruapehu**, **Ngauruhoe**, and others stood tall, each with their own power.

But they began to quarrel over who was the greatest.

---

## 🗡️ The Challenge

The mountains decided to settle the dispute with a battle. Tongariro, the most ancient, stood firm. Taranaki, proud and beautiful, challenged him.

They hurled rocks and spewed fire. The earth shook. Valleys were carved and hills flattened.

For days the battle raged. One by one, the other mountains joined or fled.

---

## 🏆 Tongariro’s Victory

In the end, Tongariro proved strongest. His eruptions were fiercest, his heart most stubborn. Taranaki was defeated.

In shame and anger, Taranaki tore himself from the ground. He dragged himself westward, carving a great gouge that became the Whanganui River. He travelled until he reached the coast, where he stands alone today—Mount Taranaki.

Ruapehu and Ngauruhoe stayed close to Tongariro, but they never forgot the fight. Sometimes they still rumble, reminding us of that ancient battle.

---

## 💡 What This Story Teaches

This story tells us:

> Pride can lead to conflict.  
> Even when we lose, we can find a new place to stand.  
> The land remembers the stories of its ancestors.
