# 🎵🐦 The Story of the Tūī and the Kōmako 🐦🎵

In the ancient forests, all the birds sang the same plain song. They chirped and called, but none had a beautiful voice. The god **Tāne**, who loved the forest, decided to give the birds a gift.

He called them together and said, “I will give you voices that can sing. Each of you will sing differently, and your songs will fill the world with joy.”

---

## 🎁 Choosing the Song

Tāne told the birds to fly up to a special tree, the **kōmako** tree, where he had placed the gift of song. The first bird to reach the tree would choose the best song.

All the birds raced toward the tree. But the **tūī** was clever. Instead of flying straight, he hid in the bushes and watched.

The little **kōmako** (a small forest bird) was the first to reach the tree. Tāne was about to give him the gift when the tūī flew out of hiding.

---

## 🧠 A Clever Trick

“Wait!” cried the tūī. “I was here first. I was just resting in the bushes.”

Tāne saw the tūī’s trick. He was disappointed, but he let the tūī choose first anyway.

The tūī greedily took the finest song—a voice that could imitate any bird, any sound, any melody. He flew away, singing with pride.

---

## 🌿 The Kōmako’s Reward

When the kōmako finally received his song, it was simple. But Tāne saw the kōmako’s patient heart. He said, “You were first in effort, even if not in trickery. Your song will be plain, but you will be known for your gentle call and your hard work. And you will be the only bird who can sing to the kōmako tree.”

The kōmako accepted his gift with grace.

---

## 💡 What This Story Teaches

This story reminds us:

> Cheating may get you ahead, but it does not earn respect.  
> Patience and honesty are true virtues.  
> Every voice has its own beauty and purpose.
