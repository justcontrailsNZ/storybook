# 🌊🐉 The Legend of the Taniwha Hotupuku 🐉🌊

In the lakes and rivers of the central North Island, there lived a taniwha named **Hotupuku**. He was enormous, with scales like stone and eyes that glowed in the dark. Hotupuku would rise from the water and snatch people who came too close to the shore.

The people of the nearby villages lived in terror. They could not fish, swim, or even gather water without fear.

---

## 🛶 A Young Warrior’s Quest

A young warrior named **Tūkāro** decided that something must be done. He had lost his father to the taniwha and was determined to end the creature’s reign of fear.

Tūkāro sought the advice of a wise tohunga (priest). The tohunga told him: “Hotupuku’s only weakness is his hunger. If you can make him chase you, you can lead him to a trap.”

---

## 🧠 The Plan

Tūkāro built a strong net of flax and wove spells into the ropes. He placed the net across a narrow channel where the taniwha often passed.

Then, he stood at the water’s edge and called out: “Hotupuku! Coward! Come and face me!”

The water churned. Hotupuku rose, roaring with fury. Tūkāro ran, leading the taniwha toward the net.

---

## 🎣 The Capture

The taniwha burst through the channel, and the net closed around him. Tūkāro and the villagers pulled the ropes tight. Hotupuku struggled, but the spells held. They trapped him in a deep pool where he could no longer harm anyone.

The people celebrated. They named the place **Te Waiāriki**—the water of the trap—and lived in peace from that day.

---

## 💡 What This Story Teaches

This story teaches:

> Courage and cleverness can overcome great danger.  
> Wisdom from elders can guide us in hard times.  
> Standing up for your people is a noble act.
