# 🔥🌱 Māui and the Magic Fire 🌱🔥

Long ago, people did not know how to make fire. They ate their food raw and shivered in the cold. Māui, the clever ancestor, saw how hard life was and decided to find the secret of fire.

He knew that his grandmother, **Mahuika**, was the goddess of fire. She lived deep in a cave at the end of the world, surrounded by flames. Māui set out on a journey to visit her.

---

## 🧭 A Long Journey

Māui walked for many days. He crossed mountains and rivers. Finally, he reached the cave where Mahuika lived. The heat from the entrance made him sweat.

Mahuika greeted him with a flicker of surprise. “Grandson, why have you come?”

“Grandmother,” Māui said politely, “I need fire. My people are cold and hungry.”

Mahuika was fond of Māui. She pulled one of her fingernails, which glowed like a burning ember, and gave it to him. “Take this,” she said. “Guard it well.”

---

## 🧠 A Clever Trick

Māui thanked her and started back. But instead of carrying the fire carefully, he played with it. He threw it in the air. He dropped it into a stream to see it hiss. He let it burn out.

When the fire was gone, Māui returned to Mahuika.

“Grandmother, I lost the fire. Could I have another?”

Mahuika frowned but gave him another glowing fingernail.

Māui did the same thing again—and again. Each time he returned, asking for more. Mahuika began to suspect he was playing tricks.

Finally, when Māui came back once more, Mahuika was furious. “You have been wasting my fire!” she roared.

---

## 🔥 A Chase of Fire

Mahuika threw her last, biggest fingernail at Māui. The whole world seemed to burst into flame. Māui ran for his life, with fire chasing him from behind.

He called to his ancestor, Tāwhirimātea, the god of weather. Rain poured down. Hail fell. The flames hissed and shrank. Māui escaped, but the fire had scorched the land and left some trees black.

Māui looked at the burnt ground and felt sorry. He understood that fire was not a toy.

---

## 💡 Fire for Everyone

From that day, people learned to make fire by rubbing sticks together. Mahuika had hidden the secret inside trees—if you rub two pieces of wood, you can still make sparks.

Māui’s adventure taught everyone:

> Fire is powerful and must be treated with respect.  
> Playing with something precious can have dangerous results.  
> Sometimes we learn lessons the hard way.
