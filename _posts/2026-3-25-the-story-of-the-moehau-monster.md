# 🏞️🐉 The Story of the Moehau Monster 🐉🏞️

In the rugged mountains of the Coromandel Peninsula stands **Moehau**, the highest peak. According to legend, it is the home of a fearsome taniwha named **Moehau**—a creature with the body of a lizard and the wings of a bird.

The taniwha was a guardian of the mountain. It was said to snatch any who ventured too close to its lair, especially those who disrespected the land.

---

## 🧗 The Hunters’ Mistake

Two hunters once climbed Moehau in search of birds. They ignored the warnings of the elders and carried weapons into the sacred forest. As they reached the summit, they boasted loudly about their skill.

Suddenly, a shadow passed over them. The taniwha swooped down, its wings blocking the sun. The hunters ran, but they became lost in the mist. They were never seen again.

---

## 🏔️ Respecting the Guardian

From that day, the people taught their children to approach the mountain with humility. If they needed food or resources from the forest, they would first ask permission and leave offerings.

Sometimes, hikers still report strange sounds—a low growl, or the beating of huge wings. The legend lives on, reminding everyone that the mountains have guardians who must be respected.

---

## 💡 What This Story Teaches

This story reminds us:

> The natural world has its own guardians.  
> Pride and carelessness can lead to danger.  
> Respecting the land keeps us safe.
