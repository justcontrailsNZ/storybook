# 🌋💧 The Legend of Lake Taupō 💧🌋

Long ago, the great mountains of the central North Island were not as they are today. The high priest **Ngātoro-i-rangi** had climbed Mount Tongariro to claim it for his people. But a rival tribe sent enemies to chase him.

Ngātoro-i-rangi was trapped on the mountain, freezing in the snow. He called to his sisters in Hawaiki to send fire. They sent fire in the form of taniwha, which travelled underground, erupting at **Whakarewarewa** and **Rotokawa**, then at the edge of a great empty basin.

---

## 🔥 The Fire That Filled a Lake

The fire reached Ngātoro-i-rangi just in time, warming him on the summit. But the last eruption was so powerful that it shook the land. The great basin collapsed, and water rushed in to fill it—creating **Lake Taupō**.

The people named the lake **Taupō-nui-a-Tia** after the explorer Tia, who first saw its vast waters. They believed the lake was sacred, for it was born from fire and water together.

---

## 🏔️ The Mountain’s Connection

Some say that the lake’s waters are still heated by the underground fires that saved Ngātoro-i-rangi. Hot springs bubble up along its shores, and the mountain stands watch, forever linked to the lake by the journey of the sacred fire.

---

## 💡 What This Story Teaches

This story teaches:

> Fire and water are powerful forces that shape the land.  
> Courage and connection to ancestors can save us.  
> The natural features we see today were created by ancient events.
