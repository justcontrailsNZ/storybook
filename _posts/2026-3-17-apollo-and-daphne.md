# 🌞🏹 The Myth of Apollo and Daphne 🌿🏹

Long ago, the god **Apollo** was known for many things: music, archery, and bright sunlight. Apollo was proud of his talents and liked to win.

One day, Apollo made fun of another god, **Eros**, who used small arrows to spark feelings in people.

> “Your arrows are tiny,” he said. “My bow is for real heroes.”

Eros didn’t argue. He simply decided to teach Apollo a lesson about pride.

---

## 🏹 Two Arrows

Eros took out two arrows. One was gold and shone brightly. The other was dull and grey.

Eros aimed the golden arrow at Apollo. Then he aimed the grey arrow at a forest **nymph** (a nature spirit) named **Daphne**, who loved being free in the woods and did not want anyone to chase her or control her.

The arrows flew silently.

Suddenly, Apollo felt a strong desire to find Daphne and talk to her. He thought about her all the time.

At the same time, Daphne felt the opposite. She wanted to be left alone. She wanted to run, explore, and choose her own path.

---

## 🌲 A Chase Through the Forest

Apollo saw Daphne in the forest and called out, “Daphne! Wait!”

Daphne looked over her shoulder and felt alarmed. She turned and ran.

Apollo ran after her. “I only want to speak,” he called. “Stop!”

But Daphne didn’t feel safe. She sprinted through trees and over rocky ground. Her hair flew behind her, and her feet moved fast.

Apollo was quick too. He was a god, and his legs did not tire like human legs.

Daphne could hear him getting closer.

---

## 🌊 Daphne Asks for Help

Daphne reached a river and cried out to her father, a river god.

> “Help me!” she begged. “Please protect me. I want to stay free.”

Her father heard her and answered. The ground trembled gently, and Daphne began to change.

Her feet rooted into the earth. Her arms became branches. Her hair became leaves.

**Daphne turned into a laurel tree.**

Apollo arrived and stopped, shocked. He reached out, then pulled his hand back.

He could not chase a tree.

---

## 😔 Apollo’s Regret

Apollo stood quietly for a long moment. He felt ashamed. For the first time, Apollo realised his pride had pushed him too far.

> “I am sorry,” Apollo said. “I did not listen.”

---

## 🍃 The Laurel Tree

Apollo decided the **laurel** (a leafy tree) would be special to him. He took laurel leaves and made a crown, not to show off, but as a reminder.

Whenever Apollo saw laurel leaves, he would remember Daphne’s choice and the importance of **respect** (treating others properly).

---

✨  ✨  ✨

## 💡 What the Myth Teaches

> **Power without respect hurts. Always listen to others.**

This myth is remembered for its message: it is not enough to be powerful or talented. You must also be respectful.

It also reminds readers that everyone deserves to feel safe, and everyone deserves to be heard.
