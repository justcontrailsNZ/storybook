# 🏞️⛵ The Legend of the Hokianga Harbour ⛵🏞️

After Kupe discovered Aotearoa, he travelled up and down the coast, naming places and leaving his mark. One day, he came to a great harbour on the west coast of the North Island. It was wide and deep, with sand dunes at its entrance.

Kupe decided to explore the harbour. He paddled his canoe, **Nukutere**, through the entrance and into the calm waters beyond.

---

## 🐚 A Sad Farewell

Inside the harbour, Kupe’s daughter, **Hokianga**, became very ill. The family cared for her, but she did not recover. She died in that place, and Kupe was heartbroken.

He named the harbour **Te Hokianga-nui-a-Kupe**—the great returning place of Kupe—to honour his daughter and to mark the place where he would turn back to Hawaiki.

---

## 🌊 The Sandbar

Before Kupe left, he cast two sand dunes across the harbour entrance. One was for the incoming tide, the other for the outgoing. He said, “These will protect those who enter and guide them safely home.”

The dunes remain today, and the entrance to Hokianga Harbour is still guarded by two great sandbars. Sailors know to watch the tides and respect the waka of Kupe.

---

## 💡 Remembering Hokianga

The people who later settled near the harbour remember Kupe and his daughter. They tell the story to teach:

> Even great explorers feel sorrow.  
> Places are named to honour those we love.  
> The land holds memories that last forever.
