# 💚🗡️ The Legend of Pounamu 🗡️💚

Long ago, there lived a great sea creature named **Poutini**, who was a **taniwha** (a powerful guardian). Poutini’s skin was smooth and green, like the deep ocean. He swam the waters around Aotearoa, protecting the people of the coast.

One day, Poutini saw a beautiful woman named **Waitaiki** bathing in a stream. She was the wife of a chief from the North Island. Poutini was so taken by her beauty that he swept her away, carrying her south on his back.

---

## 🏃‍♂️ The Chief’s Pursuit

When the chief discovered his wife was gone, he was filled with rage. He gathered his warriors and began to chase Poutini. They followed the coast, searching for any sign of the taniwha.

Poutini, sensing the danger, fled further and further south. But the chief was relentless. He would not give up.

Finally, Poutini reached the South Island. He knew he could not escape forever. To protect Waitaiki from being taken back—and to save her from the cold—he made a heartbreaking choice.

---

## 💚 The Transformation

Poutini used his magic to transform Waitaiki into a form that would last forever. He laid her body in the river and covered her with his own green scales. She became **pounamu**—greenstone—hard, beautiful, and everlasting.

The chief arrived too late. All he found was the green stone gleaming in the water. He wept, for he had lost his wife forever.

---

## 🌊 Pounamu Today

From that day, the rivers of the South Island carried pounamu. It became a treasured taonga (treasure), used for tools, weapons, and jewellery. People believe that pounamu carries the spirit of Waitaiki and the power of Poutini.

---

## 💡 What This Story Teaches

This story tells us:

> Pounamu is not just stone—it holds memory and meaning.  
> Love can lead to great joy or great sorrow.  
> The land and its treasures are woven with stories of our ancestors.

## 📖 Quick Glossary

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **taniwha** | a powerful water spirit or guardian |
| **pounamu** | greenstone, a hard and beautiful stone found in the South Island |
| **relentless** | not giving up |
| **taonga** | treasure or precious thing |
