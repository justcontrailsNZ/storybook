title: "Callisto and the Bear"
---


Long ago, there was a girl named Callisto who loved the forest. She was brave, quiet, and happiest under tall trees with sunlight flickering through the leaves.

Callisto spent her days outdoors, running over hills and listening to birds. She felt at home in nature.

But in Greek myths, the gods sometimes caused problems for humans, especially when they became jealous.

A jealous anger
A powerful goddess became angry with Callisto. (Different versions tell this part differently, but the result is the same.)

Callisto did not understand why trouble was coming. She only knew her life suddenly felt unsafe and confusing.

Then, with a burst of magic, Callisto was changed into a bear.

Callisto looked down at her paws and fur and felt terrified.

“I’m still me,” she thought. “But no one will know.”

She ran deep into the forest and hid. She learned how to find berries and water and how to stay away from people.

Even though she was now a bear, Callisto still felt human feelings: loneliness, fear, and hope.

A dangerous meeting
Years passed.

One day, a group of hunters came into the forest. Callisto heard their voices and smelled their smoke. She tried to keep away.

But then she saw a young hunter who looked familiar. In some versions, he was her son, grown up and not knowing who she was.

Callisto stepped forward slowly, wanting to show she was not dangerous.

But to the hunters, she looked like a wild bear.

They raised their spears.

Callisto froze. She wanted to speak, but bears cannot speak.

A rescue in the sky
At the last moment, a god saw what was happening and felt sorry for Callisto. To save her, Callisto was lifted up into the sky and placed among the stars.

She became a constellation called Ursa Major, which means “the Great Bear.”

In some stories, another bear constellation, Ursa Minor (the Little Bear), was placed nearby so Callisto would not be alone.

Why people remember it
Greek people used this story to explain why there are bear-shaped star patterns in the night sky.

The myth also reminds us: you cannot always tell who someone is by what they look like. It is wise to pause, think, and be careful before you judge.

Quick glossary: jealous (upset because you want what someone else has), hunter (someone who hunts animals), constellation (star pattern), judge (decide what someone is like)
