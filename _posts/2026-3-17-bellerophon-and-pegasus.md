title: "Bellerophon and Pegasus"
---


Long ago, there was a young man named Bellerophon. He wanted to be a hero, but he was not famous yet. He was still learning how to be brave and how to make good choices.

In the land where Bellerophon lived, people whispered about a magical horse named Pegasus. Pegasus was a beautiful white horse with huge wings. He could fly above mountains and clouds.

Many had tried to catch Pegasus. No one succeeded.

Bellerophon decided, “I will try.”

A helpful guide
Bellerophon travelled to a temple of Athena and asked for help.

“Goddess Athena,” he said, “I want to do something good, not just something impressive. Please guide me.”

That night, Bellerophon dreamed Athena gave him a special golden bridle (a strap used to guide a horse).

When Bellerophon woke up, the golden bridle was in his hands.

He knew what to do next.

Meeting Pegasus
Bellerophon searched until he found Pegasus drinking from a clear spring. The winged horse lifted its head and watched him carefully.

Bellerophon’s heart thumped. Pegasus looked strong and fast. One kick could send a person flying.

Bellerophon remembered Athena’s help. He walked slowly and calmly.

“It’s okay,” he whispered.

When Pegasus moved, Bellerophon did not chase wildly. He waited for the right moment, then gently placed the bridle over Pegasus’ head.

To Bellerophon’s surprise, Pegasus did not fight. It was as if the bridle helped Pegasus trust him.

Bellerophon climbed onto Pegasus’ back. Pegasus spread his wings.

With one powerful jump, they lifted into the air.

Bellerophon gasped as the ground dropped away. The wind rushed past his ears. Below him, trees looked like tiny dots.

“I’m flying,” he whispered.

A hero’s task
Soon, Bellerophon was given a dangerous job. A fierce monster called the Chimera was hurting villages. The Chimera was said to be a strange mix of animals, and it breathed fire.

People were frightened. They stayed inside. They stopped travelling.

Bellerophon wanted to help.

On Pegasus, he could fly high above the monster and avoid its flames. He planned carefully and stayed alert.

From the sky, Bellerophon spotted the Chimera. Pegasus swooped and climbed, fast as a storm cloud.

Bellerophon did not rush in without thinking. He waited until the monster was in the right place, then acted.

With Pegasus’ speed and Bellerophon’s courage, the Chimera was defeated, and the villages were safe again.

A warning about pride
Bellerophon became famous. People cheered for him wherever he went.

But fame can be tricky. After a while, Bellerophon began to think he was more than human.

He said, “I am so great that I should fly to the home of the gods.”

That was not a wise idea. In Greek myths, the home of the gods was not a place for humans.

Bellerophon tried to ride Pegasus higher and higher. Pegasus did not want to go.

Bellerophon lost control and fell back down to Earth. He survived, but he was badly hurt and very lonely.

Bellerophon learned too late: courage is good, but pride can ruin a hero.

Quick glossary: bridle (straps to guide a horse), Chimera (a fire-breathing monster), fame (being well-known), pride (thinking you are better than others)
