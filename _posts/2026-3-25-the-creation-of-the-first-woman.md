# 🌱👧 The Creation of the First Woman 👧🌱

After Tāne separated the earth and sky, the world was filled with forests, birds, and light. But one thing was missing—human beings. Tāne wanted to create people who could live on Papatūānuku and care for the land.

He asked his brothers for help. His brother Tūmatauenga gave him the idea to shape a being from the sacred earth. Tāne travelled to Kurawaka, a special place where the earth was rich and red.

---

## 🏺 Moulding the First Woman

At Kurawaka, Tāne knelt and scooped up red clay. He shaped it carefully—making a head, arms, a body, and legs. He worked with patience, breathing life into the form.

When he finished, he had created a woman. He named her **Hineahuone** (Earth-formed Woman).

But Hineahuone lay still. She was beautiful but lifeless. Tāne knew she needed the breath of life.

---

## 💨 The Breath of Life

Tāne called upon the winds of the heavens. He took the breath from his own nose and pressed his nose to hers—the **hongi**—and breathed into her.

Hineahuone’s chest rose. Her eyes opened. She stood up and looked at the world with wonder.

She was the first human woman. From her, all people would descend.

---

## 👪 A New Beginning

Hineahuone and Tāne had a daughter, also named Hineahuone (or sometimes Hinetītama). That daughter grew and later became Hinenuitepō, the goddess of night and death. But that is another story.

This story tells us:

> Humans are connected to the earth—we come from Papatūānuku.  
> Life is a gift, and it is shared through breath and love.  
> The first woman was created with care and respect.

## 📖 Quick Glossary

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **sacred** | holy and special |
| **moulding** | shaping with your hands |
| **hongi** | the Māori greeting of pressing noses to share breath |
| **descend** | to come from an ancestor |
