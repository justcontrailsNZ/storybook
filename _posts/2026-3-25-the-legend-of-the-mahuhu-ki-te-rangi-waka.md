# ☁️⛵ The Legend of the Māhuhu-ki-te-rangi Waka ⛵☁️

The canoe **Māhuhu-ki-te-rangi** was one of the earliest to reach Aotearoa. It was captained by **Rongomai**, a chief who had dreamed of a new land. The waka was named after the cloud that guided it—a great white cloud that appeared in the sky, leading them south.

When the canoe first sighted the North Island, the crew saw the Kaipara Harbour. They entered and found a land rich with forests, fish, and birds.

---

## 🌿 A Place of Plenty

Rongomai and his people settled along the Kaipara Harbour and the west coast. They became known for their skills as navigators and fishermen. The waka itself was preserved, and its stories were passed down through generations.

The descendants of the Māhuhu-ki-te-rangi spread across the north, becoming the Ngāti Whātua, Te Roroa, and other iwi who still honour the waka today.

---

## 🕊️ The Cloud That Remains

It is said that the cloud that guided the waka never fully disappeared. On misty mornings over the Kaipara Harbour, people sometimes see a single white cloud resting above the water—a reminder of the journey that brought their ancestors home.

---

## 💡 What This Story Teaches

This story teaches:

> Guidance can come from unexpected places—even the sky.  
> The journeys of our ancestors connect us to the land.  
> We honour the past by remembering and sharing its stories.
