# 🌋🦎 Ihenga and the Discovery of Rotorua 🦎🌋

Ihenga was an explorer from the Te Arawa canoe. He travelled through the North Island, discovering new lands and giving names to places.

One day, while hunting, he came across a giant lizard—a **kawekaweau**. The lizard was so enormous that its body stretched across the land.

---

## 🦎 Following the Lizard

Ihenga followed the lizard as it crawled through the forest. It led him to a steaming valley filled with boiling pools and bubbling mud.

When the lizard disappeared into a cave, Ihenga continued alone. He climbed a ridge and saw something amazing: a great lake, its waters warm and shimmering in the sun.

---

## 🏞️ The Lake and the Springs

Ihenga named the lake **Rotorua**—the “second lake” because he had already discovered another lake earlier. He also saw the hot springs, which he called Whakarewarewa.

He returned to his people with news of the warm lake and geothermal wonders. The Te Arawa people settled there and made Rotorua their home.

---

## 💡 What This Story Teaches

This story reminds us:

> Exploration often requires patience and curiosity.  
> Following unexpected paths can lead to great discoveries.  
> The land holds many gifts for those who seek them.
