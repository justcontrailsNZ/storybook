# 🌸🌊 The Story of the Pohutukawa Tree 🌊🌸

There was once a young warrior named **Tawhaki**. He was brave and strong, and he loved his people dearly. One day, while trying to rescue his wife from enemies, he was wounded.

Tawhaki fell to the ground, and his blood stained the earth. Where his blood fell, a new tree began to grow.

---

## 🌺 The Red Flowers

The tree grew tall and strong. Its branches spread wide, and its roots clung to rocky cliffs. When summer came, the tree burst into bloom with bright red flowers—the colour of Tawhaki’s blood and courage.

People called it **pōhutukawa**, the tree of the coast.

---

## 🎄 A Sacred Symbol

For generations, Māori have treasured the pōhutukawa. Its wood was used for tools and weapons. Its flowers were a sign that summer had arrived.

The tree became a symbol of strength, standing firm against wind and waves. People said that the spirits of ancestors travel along its roots to the sea, beginning their journey to the afterlife.

---

## 💡 What the Pōhutukawa Teaches

This tree teaches us:

> Strength can grow from sacrifice.  
> Even in tough places, life can flourish.  
> Nature connects us to our ancestors and our stories.
