Long ago, the gods created the first woman, named Pandora. She was clever, curious, and full of questions about the world.

Pandora was given many gifts, but she was also given something else: a special container. Some stories say it was a box, and some stories say it was a jar.

It looked ordinary on the outside, but it felt important.

Pandora was told one clear rule:

“Do not open it.”

Pandora promised she would not.

The container on the shelf
Pandora took the container home and placed it on a shelf where she could see it. At first, she did not think about it much.

But as days passed, Pandora started to wonder.

“What could be inside?” she thought. “Why would the gods give me something I can’t open?”

Every time she walked past the shelf, her eyes landed on the lid.

She tried to distract herself. She cleaned. She visited friends. She went for walks.

But the question stayed in her mind like a buzzing fly.

Curiosity grows
One afternoon, Pandora sat staring at the container. The room was quiet. Outside, the wind moved the leaves.

Pandora’s thoughts grew louder.

“Just one look,” she whispered to herself. “Just a tiny peek. I won’t touch anything.”

Her hands reached up before she could stop them.

Pandora lifted the lid a small amount.

Trouble escapes
WHOOSH!

A dark rush burst out of the container like a storm.

Pandora jumped back, startled. Shadows swirled through the air and flew out the window and into the world.

Pandora heard sounds like crying, shouting, coughing, and arguing.

She realised, with a sick feeling in her stomach, that terrible things had escaped: sadness, sickness, anger, jealousy, and fear.

Pandora slammed the lid shut with shaking hands.

“Oh no,” she gasped. “What have I done?”

She sat down, feeling ashamed and upset.

One last thing inside
For a while, Pandora stayed still, listening. The world outside seemed louder than before, as if people were suddenly dealing with problems they had never had.

Then Pandora heard a small sound from inside the container.

It wasn’t like the other sounds. It was softer—like a gentle whisper.

Pandora leaned closer. “Is something still in there?” she murmured.

Her hands trembled, but she slowly lifted the lid again—only a crack.

This time, something light and bright floated out. It drifted into the air like a warm glow.

It was Hope.

Pandora watched it float away, and she felt a tiny bit of comfort.

The meaning of the myth
This myth does not say that cu riosity is always bad. Curiosity helps people learn, explore, and discover.

But the story reminds us that some choices cannot be undone.

Even when troubles enter the world, Hope reminds people to keep going, to help each other, and to believe that good things can still happen.

Pandora learned that day that rules can exist for a reason—and that it is wise to stop and think before acting.

Quick glossary (word help)
curiosity: wanting to know more
escaped: got out and ran free
troubles: problems and hard times
hope: believing things can get better
