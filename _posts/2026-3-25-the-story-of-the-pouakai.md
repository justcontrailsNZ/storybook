# 🦅⛰️ The Story of the Pouākai ⛰️🦅

In ancient times, a giant bird called the **pouākai** soared above the South Island. It was the largest eagle the world had ever seen, with wings that stretched wider than a waka was long. Its talons were sharp as spears, and its beak could crush bones.

The pouākai was a fearsome hunter. It preyed on the great moa birds, but sometimes it would attack people. Villages lived in fear.

---

## 🏹 The Warriors’ Plan

The people tried to fight the pouākai, but their spears could not reach it in the air. They built traps, but the bird was too clever.

Finally, a group of warriors devised a plan. They would hide in a cave and wait for the pouākai to land. One warrior, **Rangi**, would act as bait, standing in the open until the eagle swooped down.

---

## ⚔️ The Battle

The pouākai saw Rangi and dove toward him. Rangi ran toward the cave, and the bird followed, its claws outstretched. As it entered the cave mouth, the other warriors sprang out, striking the great eagle with their weapons.

The battle was fierce. The pouākai fought with fury, but the warriors were brave. In the end, the bird fell, and the people were safe.

---

## 💡 Remembering the Giant

From that day, the people told stories of the pouākai. They carved its image into the wood of their meeting houses to remind themselves that even the most powerful creatures can be overcome with courage and teamwork.

---

## 💡 What This Story Teaches

This story reminds us:

> Even the bravest warriors need a plan.  
> Teamwork can defeat even the mightiest enemy.  
> Facing fear together makes us stronger.
