# 🦉🌳 How the Kiwi Lost Its Wings 🌳🦉

Long ago, the forests of Aotearoa were filled with birds. Tāne, the god of the forest, was their guardian. He looked after the trees, the plants, and all the creatures that lived there.

But a terrible problem was growing. Pests were eating the forest from the ground up. Caterpillars munched on leaves. Bugs ate the roots. The trees began to grow weak, and Tāne was worried.

“If this continues,” Tāne said, “the forest will die. I need someone to live on the ground and protect it.”

He called all the birds together.

---

## 🪶 The Meeting of the Birds

The birds gathered in the tallest treetop. There was Pīwakawaka the fantail, Ruru the morepork, Tūī the singer, and many others. Tāne looked at them and explained the problem.

“The forest needs a guardian,” he said. “I need one of you to leave the safety of the trees and live on the forest floor. You will eat the pests and keep the trees healthy.”

A great silence fell.

One by one, the birds spoke.

Pīwakawaka fluttered nervously. “I love to dance in the sunlight. The ground is too dark and damp.”

Ruru blinked his big eyes. “I prefer the night, but I like to perch high in the branches. I’m sorry, Tāne.”

Tūī shook her glossy feathers. “I spend my days singing from the treetops. I cannot live on the ground.”

One by one, each bird made an excuse. The forest floor seemed too dark, too dangerous, too lonely.

---

## 💚 The Kiwi Steps Forward

Then a small voice spoke.

“I will do it.”

It was the kiwi. He had stubby legs, a long beak, and soft, brown feathers. He looked at Tāne with kind eyes.

“The forest is my home too,” the kiwi said. “If the trees die, we all lose our home. I will live on the ground and protect it.”

Tāne was moved by the kiwi’s courage. He warned him, “If you go to the forest floor, you will never see sunlight again. Your wings will become small. Your feathers will grow thick to keep you warm, but you will lose your colour. Are you still willing?”

The kiwi nodded. “I am.”

Tāne thanked the kiwi. Then, as a reward for his sacrifice, Tāne promised that the kiwi would be the most beloved bird of Aotearoa. People would recognise its bravery for generations.

---

## 🏆 A New Guardian

The kiwi climbed down from the trees and settled on the forest floor. True to Tāne’s words, his wings grew small and useless for flying. His feathers turned brown to blend in with the leaves. He grew strong legs for digging and a long beak for finding bugs.

He spent his days and nights protecting the forest. And slowly, the trees began to heal.

When other birds saw the kiwi’s dedication, they felt ashamed of their excuses. But Tāne reminded them that every bird has a role, and the kiwi’s role was special.

---

## 💡 What This Story Teaches

This story is told to remind us:

> Sometimes the most important jobs are not the ones that look glamorous.  
> True bravery means doing what needs to be done, even when it’s hard.  
> Caring for our home and others can be the greatest gift of all.

## 📖 Quick Glossary

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **guardian** | someone who protects and cares for something |
| **pests** | creatures that cause harm to plants or crops |
| **sacrifice** | giving up something important for a good reason |
| **Aotearoa** | the Māori name for New Zealand |
