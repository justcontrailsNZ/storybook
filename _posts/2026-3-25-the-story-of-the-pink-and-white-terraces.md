# 🌸🤍 The Story of the Pink and White Terraces 🤍🌸

Long before the earth shook and changed the land, there was a place of breathtaking beauty on the shores of Lake Rotomahana. Hot water from deep within the earth cascaded down hillsides, leaving behind shimmering terraces of pink and white stone. They were called **Te Tarata** (the White Terraces) and **Ōtūkapuarangi** (the Pink Terraces).

These terraces were sacred to the local iwi, the Tūhourangi people. They believed the terraces were formed by the goddesses of the springs, who used the waters to bathe and sing.

---

## 🌿 A Place of Healing

The terraces were not only beautiful—they were also a place of healing. The warm waters were said to have special powers. People came from far away to bathe in the pools, to soothe their aches and to listen to the songs of the goddesses.

Visitors were welcomed, but they were told to treat the place with respect. Some say that if anyone was careless or greedy, the waters would turn cold and the terraces would lose their colour.

---

## 🌋 A Day of Fire

One morning in 1886, the earth began to rumble. Mount Tarawera split open, throwing ash and fire into the sky. The great eruption buried the terraces under meters of mud and ash. When the dust settled, the Pink and White Terraces were gone—hidden beneath the earth.

The people mourned the loss of their sacred place. For generations, they told stories of the terraces, keeping their memory alive.

---

## 🔍 Remembering the Terraces

In recent years, explorers have searched for the terraces deep beneath the lake. Though they remain hidden, the story of their beauty and loss teaches us:

> Nature can create wonders, but it can also reclaim them.  
> We must honour and care for the places that are sacred.  
> Even when something is lost, its memory can inspire us.
