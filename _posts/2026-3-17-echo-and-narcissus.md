title: "Echo and Narcissus "
---

Long ago, in the hills and forests of Greece, there lived a nymph named Echo. In Greek myths, nymphs are nature spirits who live near mountains, trees, and streams.

Echo was friendly and loved talking to people. She told stories, asked questions, and laughed loudly.

But Echo was given a strange punishment: she could not speak first.

From that day on, Echo could only repeat the last words she heard.

It made life very difficult.

Meeting Narcissus
One day, a young man named Narcissus walked through Echo’s forest. Narcissus was handsome, and he knew it. He was proud and often unkind.

Echo saw him and felt excited. She wanted to say hello.

But she couldn’t start.

Narcissus heard a sound in the trees and called out, “Is anyone there?”

Echo’s voice answered, “Anyone there…”

Narcissus looked around, confused. “Come here!”

Echo repeated, “Come here!”

Echo stepped out from behind the trees, smiling. She hoped Narcissus would be pleased to meet her.

But Narcissus frowned.

He did not ask her name. He did not try to understand her strange problem. He only cared about himself.

“Go away,” Narcissus said, and he walked off.

Echo stood still, feeling hurt and embarrassed. She had wanted friendship, but she had been treated like she didn’t matter.

Echo fades away
Echo became quieter and quieter. She hid in caves and lonely places. She stopped trying to meet people.

In some versions of the myth, Echo slowly faded until only her voice remained.

That is why, in empty places like caves or mountains, you can sometimes hear a sound repeat back to you.

It is called an echo.

Narcissus and the pond
Later, Narcissus became thirsty and stopped at a clear pond to drink. The water was still, like glass.

When Narcissus leaned over, he saw a face in the water.

His own face.

But Narcissus didn’t understand it was a reflection. He thought it was someone else—someone beautiful.

He stared and stared.

“I have never seen anyone so perfect,” he whispered.

Narcissus forgot about everything else. He forgot to walk on. He forgot to eat. He forgot that the world was bigger than his own appearance.

He spent too long gazing at himself, and it brought him sadness instead of happiness.

The lesson
This myth teaches two important ideas:

Kindness matters. Echo needed understanding, not rejection.
Too much pride can harm you. Narcissus cared so much about himself that he stopped living a real life.
People remembered the story as a warning: admire yourself a little, but do not become so self-focused that you forget others.

Quick glossary (word help)
nymph: a nature spirit in Greek myths
punishment: a consequence for something you did
reflection: an image of yourself in water or a mirror
pride: feeling very pleased with yourself (too much can be a problem)
