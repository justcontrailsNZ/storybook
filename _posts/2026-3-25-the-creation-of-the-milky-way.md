# ✨🌌 The Creation of the Milky Way 🌌✨

After Tāne separated the earth and sky, the world was filled with light. But the nights were dark, with only the moon and a few stars to guide people. Tāne decided to decorate the night sky with thousands of stars to help travellers and fishermen.

He gathered his children—the stars—and began to place them in the heavens.

---

## 🌠 Scattering the Stars

Tāne worked carefully, placing bright stars in patterns that would help people remember stories. He placed the Southern Cross to guide explorers. He set the Matariki cluster to mark the new year.

But there were so many stars. When Tāne grew tired, he took the remaining stars and tossed them across the sky like a handful of glittering dust.

---

## 🥛 The Milky River

That great river of stars became known as **Te Ikaroa** (the long fish) or, in other traditions, **Te Waka o Tānerore** (the canoe of Tānerore). Later, it was called the Milky Way.

People looked up and saw the shimmering band, and they knew it was a reminder that the world was created with care, but also with joy.

---

## 💡 What This Story Teaches

This story tells us:

> Even the smallest star has a place in the sky.  
> Creation is a mix of careful work and joyful play.  
> The stars are maps and stories for those who know how to read them.

## 📖 Quick Glossary
