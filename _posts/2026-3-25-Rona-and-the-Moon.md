# 🌙🌿 Rona and the Moon 🌿🌙

Long ago, in a village by the sea, there lived a woman named **Rona**. Rona was known for many things, but patience was not one of them. When things did not go her way, her temper flared like a crackling fire.

Rona had a kind husband who tried his best to keep her happy. He would fetch water, cook food, and do whatever she asked, hoping to avoid her sharp words.

But one evening, something happened that would change Rona forever.

---

## 🌊 A Simple Task

The sun had set, and the sky was painted with shades of orange and purple. Rona realised she was thirsty. She reached for her gourd—a hollow container for drinking water—but found it empty.

“Husband!” she called. “Fetch me some water!”

But her husband was not there. He had gone fishing and had not yet returned.

Rona sighed loudly. “Fine,” she grumbled. “I suppose I must do it myself.”

She picked up her gourd and walked down the path toward the creek. The **Marama** (moon) floated overhead, casting silver light across the ground and showing her the way.

---

## ⛈️ A Bad Temper

As Rona walked, a dark cloud drifted across the sky. It covered the moon, and suddenly the path was swallowed by darkness.

Rona could not see where she was going. Her foot caught on a root, and she stumbled, twisting her ankle and scraping her knee.

Pain shot through her leg, and Rona’s anger boiled over.

She glared up at the sky. “You useless moon!” she shouted. “How dare you leave me in the dark! **Pokokōhua!** ” she cried—a very rude insult.

The moon stopped moving.

A deep voice rumbled from the sky. “Are you speaking to me?”

“Yes, I am!” Rona screamed, shaking her fist. “Look what you made me do!”

---

## 🤲 The Moon’s Anger

The moon was not pleased. He had been watching over Rona, lighting her way, and this was how she thanked him?

Without another word, the moon began to descend. He reached down from the sky and grabbed hold of Rona.

Rona’s eyes went wide with fear. She reached out and grabbed the nearest thing she could find—a **ngaio tree** growing beside the path. She held on with all her might.

But the moon was far stronger. With a great pull, he lifted Rona into the air. The tree came out by its roots, still clutched in her hands.

Higher and higher she rose, until she was pulled right up into the sky.

---

## 🌕 Rona in the Moon

When Rona’s husband returned with his fishing canoe, he found the village quiet. He searched everywhere, but Rona was gone.

Then he looked up at the moon.

There, on the moon’s face, he could see a shape. A woman. A gourd. And a ngaio tree, its roots still dangling.

He called out to her, but Rona could only look down in silence. She had learned her lesson, but it was too late to change what had happened.

---

## 💡 Remember Rona’s Mistake

To this day, when the moon is full, people say you can still see Rona. She stands on the moon’s face with her gourd in one hand, holding onto the ngaio tree.

Her story is told to remind us of something important:

> When anger takes hold, words can hurt—and some things cannot be undone.

Even now, when arguments begin and people feel like shouting, others may say:

**“Kia mahara ki te hē o Rona.”**

*Remember Rona’s mistake.*

## 📖 Quick Glossary

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **gourd** | a hollowed-out shell or container used for drinking water |
| **Marama** | the moon (Māori word) |
| **ngaio tree** | a small tree that grows near the coast in New Zealand |
| **Pokokōhua** | a very rude insult—not a kind thing to say! |
