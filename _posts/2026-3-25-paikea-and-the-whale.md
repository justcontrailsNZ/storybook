# 🐋🌊 Paikea and the Whale 🌊🐋

Long ago in Hawaiki, there was a chief named **Uenuku** who had many sons. Among them was **Paikea**. The brothers were jealous of Paikea and planned to kill him while they were out fishing.

They took their canoe far from shore, then pushed Paikea into the sea. They left him to drown.

---

## 🐳 A Great Rescue

Paikea did not sink. He called upon his ancestors and the gods of the ocean. A mighty whale rose from the deep. It lifted Paikea onto its back and began to swim south.

For days and nights they travelled across the vast Pacific. The whale protected Paikea from storms and guided him safely.

Finally, they reached the shores of Aotearoa—New Zealand. Paikea landed at a place he called Whāngārā, on the East Coast.

---

## 🏡 A New Life

Paikea settled in his new land. He became a great chief, and his descendants—the Ngāti Porou tribe—still honour him today.

To remember his journey, the people tell this story and carve whale motifs in their meeting houses.

---

## 💡 What This Story Teaches

This story teaches:

> Even when others betray you, help can come from unexpected places.  
> Courage and faith can carry you through great trials.  
> Your ancestors and the natural world can protect you.
