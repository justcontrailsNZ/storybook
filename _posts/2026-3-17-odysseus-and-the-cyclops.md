title: "Odysseus and the Cyclops"
---


Long ago in Ancient Greece, a clever hero named Odysseus was sailing home after a long journey. He and his men were tired, hungry, and hoping for friendly land.

One day they reached an island with green hills and rocky caves. Odysseus climbed a slope and looked around.

“I see smoke,” he said. “Someone lives here. Let’s find food and water.”

Odysseus and a small group walked toward a large cave. Outside were sheep and goats, resting quietly. Inside the cave they found baskets of cheese and big jars of milk.

One sailor whispered, “Let’s grab food and leave quickly.”

But Odysseus shook his head. “We should meet the owner,” he said. “Maybe they will share.”

So they waited.

Soon the ground began to thump. A giant arrived—taller than any person. He had one huge eye in the middle of his forehead. This was a Cyclops, and his name was Polyphemus.

Polyphemus pushed a massive stone across the cave entrance, blocking the doorway like a locked gate.

Odysseus swallowed hard. They were trapped.

Polyphemus stared at the visitors. “Who are you?” he boomed. “Why are you in my cave?”

Odysseus tried to sound polite. “We are travellers,” he said. “Please be kind to us.”

But Polyphemus was not kind. He grabbed a couple of the sailors and would not let anyone leave.

Odysseus’ mind raced. “We must escape,” he thought. “But how?”

A clever idea
Odysseus noticed something important. Polyphemus was strong, but not very careful. And the Cyclops drank a lot of milk and ate a lot of food.

Odysseus had brought a strong drink with him. That night, he offered it to Polyphemus.

“Try this,” Odysseus said.

Polyphemus gulped it down. “More!” he demanded.

Odysseus gave him more. Soon, the Cyclops’ head drooped. His voice got slow and sleepy.

Before he fell asleep, Polyphemus asked, “What is your name, little man?”

Odysseus took a deep breath and said, “My name is Nobody.”

Polyphemus grunted. “Nobody… I will eat you last,” he mumbled, and then he fell asleep.

Odysseus whispered to his men, “Listen closely. We have one chance.”

The escape plan
Odysseus and his men worked together quietly. They found a thick wooden stick and sharpened the end. When Polyphemus was deeply asleep, they used the stick to hurt the Cyclops’ eye so he could not see.

Polyphemus roared in pain. He banged around the cave, shouting for help.

Other Cyclopes called from outside, “What’s wrong? Who is hurting you?”

Polyphemus yelled, “Nobody is hurting me!”

Outside, the others replied, “If nobody is hurting you, then stop shouting!” And they walked away.

Odysseus’ men were amazed. The trick had worked.

But they still had to get out. The stone door was too heavy to move.

In the morning, Polyphemus rolled the stone aside to let his sheep out. He stood at the entrance, feeling each sheep’s back to make sure the men didn’t escape.

Odysseus whispered, “We’ll go under the sheep.”

They tied themselves under the biggest sheep. When the sheep walked out, the Cyclops only felt the wool on top.

One by one, Odysseus and his men slipped outside, still holding their breath.

When they reached their ship, they untied themselves and ran.

A final mistake
As the ship sailed away, Odysseus felt proud.

He shouted back, “Cyclops! You were tricked by Odysseus!”

His men cried, “No! Don’t tell him your name!”

Too late. Polyphemus knew who had escaped. He lifted a giant rock and threw it toward the sea. It splashed close to the ship and rocked it like a toy.

Odysseus and his men rowed faster until the island disappeared.

Odysseus had escaped by being clever and brave—but he also learned that boasting can create new danger.

Quick glossary: Cyclops (a one-eyed giant), trapped (unable to leave), clever (smart), boast (to brag)
