# 🍠🌿 The Creation of the Kūmara 🌿🍠

After Tāne separated the earth and sky, the world was green with forests and full of birds. But the people needed food they could plant and harvest. **Rongo**, the god of cultivated foods, wanted to help.

He journeyed to the heavens to find special plants that could feed his people.

---

## 🌱 A Gift from the Sky

In the highest heaven, Rongo met the guardian of sacred crops. The guardian gave him a small tuber—the kūmara (sweet potato). “This will grow in the earth and feed your people,” the guardian said. “But you must treat it with care.”

Rongo brought the kūmara back to earth. He showed people how to plant it, how to tend it, and how to harvest it. The kūmara grew well, and the people were fed.

---

## 🌙 The Moon’s Help

But the kūmara was still small. Rongo prayed to **Marama**, the moon, who knew the rhythms of the seasons. Marama taught Rongo when to plant, when to harvest, and how to store the kūmara for winter.

With the moon’s guidance, the kūmara became a staple food. The people never went hungry again.

---

## 💡 What This Story Teaches

This story tells us:

> Food is a gift that comes with responsibility.  
> The moon and seasons guide our planting.  
> Sharing knowledge helps communities thrive.

## 📖 Quick Glossary

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **cultivated** | grown by people, not wild |
| **tuber** | a root that can be planted to grow a new plant |
| **staple** | a basic, important food |
| **rhythms** | regular patterns |
