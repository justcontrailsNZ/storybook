# ⚡🦉 Athena and Poseidon Name a City ⚡🦉

Long ago in Ancient Greece, a busy city grew beside the sparkling sea. Its streets were filled with fishermen mending nets, farmers bringing olives and grain to market, and children playing on the rocky slopes. The people wanted their city to become strong, wise, and well‑known.

They also wanted a special **protector**—a god who would guide them and watch over the city for all time.

Two powerful gods both wanted that honour.

One was **Poseidon**, god of the sea. He was strong and stormy, with a temper that could shake the earth and churn the waves. He carried a three‑pronged **trident**, and when he struck the ground with it, mountains trembled.

The other was **Athena**, goddess of wisdom. She was calm, thoughtful, and known for her clever ideas, skill in crafts, and careful planning. She carried a shield and a spear, but her greatest weapon was her mind.

The people didn’t know what to do. If they chose the wrong god, they worried it might cause trouble.

So a decision was made: **each god would give the city a gift. The people would choose the gift that helped them most.**

---

## 🌊 Poseidon’s Gift

On the day of the contest, the people gathered on a rocky hill overlooking the sea. They watched the sky and the waves, waiting with nervous excitement.

First, Poseidon arrived. The wind rose, whipping their hair and clothes. Waves crashed against the shore, sending spray high into the air. Poseidon strode forward, his trident gleaming, his eyes flashing like lightning.

“I will give this city power,” Poseidon announced in a voice like thunder.

He lifted his trident and struck the ground.

With a loud crack, water burst up from the rock, gushing into the air.

People gasped and rushed forward.

But when they tasted the water, their faces fell.

“It’s salty,” they said. “Like the sea itself.”

Poseidon’s gift was a saltwater spring. It was impressive—a sign of his strength over the oceans. It reminded everyone of ships, storms, and sea travel.

Some people clapped. “Poseidon is powerful!” they cheered.

But others whispered, “Can we drink it? Can we water our crops with it?”

They weren’t sure. The spring was mighty, but it didn’t seem useful for everyday life.

---

## 🌿 Athena’s Gift

Then Athena stepped forward. She did not bring a storm. She brought a calm feeling, like a clear day after rain. Her eyes were steady, and her voice was gentle but sure.

“I will give this city something useful,” Athena said.

Athena knelt and touched the ground lightly. Then she planted something small—a single seed.

At first, nothing happened. The crowd waited in silence.

Then, quickly, a tree began to grow. A strong trunk rose from the earth. Silver‑green leaves unfurled, shimmering in the sunlight. Small green fruits appeared, then slowly ripened.

It was an **olive tree**.

The crowd leaned closer, murmuring with wonder.

Athena explained, “This tree will give you olives to eat. It will give you oil for cooking and for lamps when night falls. It will give you wood to build and to trade. It will live for many years, if you care for it.”

People looked at the olive tree and understood.

This was not a gift that shouted and roared. It was a gift that could feed families, light their homes, and help the city grow strong.

---

## 🏛️ The People Choose

Now it was time to decide.

Some people still liked Poseidon’s gift. After all, the city was by the sea. Fishing, sailing, and trade were important. Poseidon’s power could protect their ships.

But most people thought carefully about Athena’s gift.

The olive tree could help in everyday life—in kitchens, workshops, and homes. It could help farmers grow food. It could be traded with other cities for things they didn’t have. It was a gift that kept giving, year after year.

So the people chose Athena.

The city was named **Athens** in her honour.

Poseidon was angry and disappointed. In some stories, he caused storms near the coast to show his frustration. The people learned that even when you make a fair choice, someone may still feel upset.

But Athens grew into a famous city, known for learning, building, and clever ideas—just like Athena.

---

## 💡 Why People Remember This Myth

This story teaches that:

> The best gift is not always the loudest or most exciting.  
> Wisdom and planning can help a community grow.  
> Useful things, like food and light, can be more important than showing off power.

Even today, olive trees grow in Greece, and the city of Athens still honours Athena as its protector.
