title: "Athena and Poseidon Name a City"
---


Long ago in Ancient Greece, there was a growing city by the sea. It had busy streets, fishermen, and farmers nearby. The people wanted their city to become strong and well-known.

They also wanted a special protector god—a god who would guide them and watch over the city.

Two powerful gods both wanted that honour.

One was Poseidon, god of the sea. He was strong and stormy, and he could shake the ocean with his trident.

The other was Athena, goddess of wisdom. She was calm, thoughtful, and known for good ideas, skills, and planning.

The people didn’t know what to do. If they chose the wrong god, they worried it might cause trouble.

So a decision was made.

Each god would give the city a gift. The people would choose the gift that helped them most.

Poseidon’s gift
On the day of the contest, the people gathered on a rocky hill. They watched the sky and sea, waiting.

First, Poseidon arrived. The wind rose and waves crashed against the shore. Poseidon strode forward, holding his trident.

“I will give this city power,” Poseidon announced.

He lifted his trident and struck the ground.

With a loud crack, water burst up from the rock.

People gasped and rushed forward.

But when they tasted the water, their faces fell.

“It’s salty,” they said. “Like the sea.”

Poseidon’s gift was a saltwater spring. It was impressive, and it showed his strength. It reminded people of ships, storms, and sea travel.

Some people clapped. “Poseidon is powerful!” they cheered.

But others whispered, “Can we drink it? Can we use it on crops?”

They weren’t sure.

Athena’s gift
Then Athena stepped forward. She did not bring a storm. She brought a calm feeling, like a clear day after rain.

“I will give this city something useful,” Athena said.

Athena knelt and touched the ground gently. Then she planted something small.

At first, it looked like nothing. But quickly, a tree grew—strong trunk, silver-green leaves, and small fruit.

It was an olive tree.

The crowd leaned closer.

Athena explained, “This tree will give you olives to eat. It will give you oil for cooking and lamps. It will give you wood. It will live for many years, if you care for it.”

People looked at the olive tree and understood.

This was not a gift that shouted and roared. It was a gift that could feed families and help the city grow.

The people choose
Now it was time to decide.

Some people still liked Poseidon’s gift because the city was near the sea. Fishing and sailing were important.

But most people thought carefully about Athena’s gift.

The olive tree could help in everyday life. It could help farmers, builders, and families. It could help the city trade with others.

So the people chose Athena.

The city was named Athens in her honour.

Poseidon was angry and disappointed. In some stories, he caused storms near the coast to show his frustration. The people learned that even when you make a fair choice, someone may still feel upset.

But Athens grew into a famous city, known for learning, building, and clever ideas—just like Athena.

Why people remember this myth
This story teaches that:

The best gift is not always the loudest or most exciting.
Wisdom and planning can help a community grow.
Useful things, like food and light, can be more important than showing off power.
